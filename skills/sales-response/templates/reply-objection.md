# 异议回复模板

> 实际使用说明：从 objection-handbook.md 中找到对应异议类型，复制适合风格的回复模板，按需修改方括号内容。

---

## 使用格式

```
[客户原话/问题]
↓
[选择的方案：A/B/C]
↓
[复制对应模板]
↓
[按客户实际情况修改方括号内容]
↓
[通过 163-email-sender 或 WhatsApp 发送]
```

---

### 价格异议 - 方案A（数据驱动）

```

Hi [姓名],

I understand budget is a consideration. Before we discuss numbers, let me share some data that might help with your evaluation:

- Average splice cycle time with our equipment: [X] minutes
- Average splice cycle with conventional methods: [Y] minutes
- At your monthly volume of [N] splices, that's [Z] hours saved per month
- Average downtime cost in your industry: $[X]/hour
- Projected payback period: [N] months

What specific numbers would be useful for your internal review? I'd be happy to put together a customized ROI analysis for your operation.

Best regards,
Wike Chen
Sale Manager | HOLO Industrial Equipment Mfg Co., Ltd
```

### Beltwin比较 - 原厂家话术

```
Subject: Re: [原邮件主题]

Hi [姓名],

Thank you for bringing up Beltwin - it's actually a relationship I can speak to directly.

Beltwin has been one of our distribution partners for over 10 years. So the equipment comes from our factory - same manufacturing line, same quality control, same engineering team.

Here's the practical difference when you buy direct from HOLO versus through a distributor:

1. **Support structure**: When you contact us, you reach our engineering team directly. When you go through Beltwin, there's a middle layer - they contact us, then relay back.

2. **Customization**: Direct customers can request modifications, special configurations, and OEM options that go through a distributor require additional approval layers.

3. **Warranty**: Direct warranty from the manufacturer. Distributor warranty means the distributor manages the claim.

4. **Response time**: Direct line to us means faster response when you need support.

Would it be helpful if I prepared a direct cost comparison between purchasing direct and through Beltwin?

Best regards,
Wike Chen
```

### 已有供应商 - 差距分析

```
Subject: Re: [原邮件主题]

Hi [姓名],

I understand - finding and qualifying new suppliers takes time, and the relationship with [current supplier] has value.

Here's a practical thought: many of our best customers came to us after years with another supplier. What they found wasn't that the old supplier was bad - it was that there were specific gaps:

1. [Gap 1 - e.g., lead time for urgent orders]
2. [Gap 2 - e.g., support for custom applications]
3. [Gap 3 - e.g., total cost including consumables]

Would it be useful if I prepared a brief comparison of these specific areas against your current setup? No pressure to switch - just want to make sure you have complete information when you make your next decision.

What aspects of your current setup have been most challenging?

Best regards,
Wike Chen
```

```
- More information about a specific aspect?
- A reference from a customer in a similar situation?
- Something else entirely?

I'm here to help you make the best decision for your operation - even if that means waiting for the right time.

Wike Chen
```
